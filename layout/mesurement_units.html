Mesurement Units in HTML:

So far we measure the element with pixel. But there are others mesurement unites as well and they fall in to two catagories, Absolute and Relative.

1. Absolute units are always fixed, the value does not change. Example are:

  • a. px(pixel)
  • b. pt(point)
  • c. in(Inch)
  • d. cm, mm and so on. The cm and mm are used in priting not in web.
2. Relative units are someting else like:
  • a. % that relative to the size of container.
  • b. vw and vh which are relative to size of the viewport.
  • c. em and rem which are relative to the font size.
